# ExampleCo Procurement Policy Guide

This is synthetic reference material for the Agent2 procurement compliance
officer example. It is intentionally small but structured like a policy book.

## Required request fields

Every purchase request must include:

- request id
- requester email
- department or cost center
- vendor name
- category
- amount and currency
- business justification

If one of these fields is missing, the officer should ask for clarification
instead of approving.

## Approval thresholds

Use the total purchase amount in USD-equivalent value.

- Under 5,000 USD: manager or department-head approval is sufficient when the
  vendor is known and the category is routine.
- 5,000 to 24,999 USD: department-head approval is required.
- 25,000 to 99,999 USD: department-head and finance approval are required.
- 100,000 USD or higher: CFO approval is required in addition to department-head
  and finance approval.

## Competitive sourcing

Purchases above 25,000 USD require evidence of competitive sourcing unless the
request includes a specific sole-source rationale.

A sole-source rationale must explain:

- why the vendor is uniquely qualified
- why substitutes are not acceptable
- whether the price was benchmarked
- who owns the vendor relationship

If a purchase above 25,000 USD claims sole-source treatment but no rationale is
provided, ask for clarification.

## Software and data review

Software purchases above 10,000 USD require security review.

Any vendor that processes employee data, customer data, source code, financial
data, or health data requires:

- security review id
- data processing agreement status
- system owner
- renewal or termination owner

If data scope is unclear, ask for clarification.

## Gifts, events, and marketing spend

Marketing event purchases must state:

- event purpose
- attendee audience
- gift value, if gifts are involved
- whether personal data will be collected

Gift value above 100 USD per attendee requires finance review. Missing attendee
or data-collection context is a clarification case.

## Rejection criteria

Reject the request when:

- the vendor is blocked by vendor-risk policy
- sanctions screening shows a match
- documentation appears fabricated
- the purchase is prohibited by policy
- the request cannot be cured by asking for more information
